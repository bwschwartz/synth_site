This page features two analog synthesizer modules models: a voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO) on the left and an amplifier on the right.

The VCO has two sets of outputs: sine waves (left) and square waves (right).

By default, the amplifier knobs control volume of the sine output on the same row of the VCO. To start making sound, press the "ON" button and turn up the amplifier knobs.

To reroute the setup, click a VCO output followd by an amplifier input. You will see a cable spring up between the two; now you can control that VCO output with the corresponding amplifier knob.

To sever any active connection, click the opposite VCO output in the same row.

The VCO emits frequencies in the 0-360 Hz range, which correspond directly to the dial position.
